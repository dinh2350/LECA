## Persona 1 — The Aspiring Professional (Primary)

**Profile**
- Age: 22–32
- Location: Vietnam, Indonesia, India, Brazil, Philippines, Mexico
- Occupation: Student, entry-to-mid level professional (IT, customer service, business)
- Income: $300–$1,000/month
- English level: A2–B1 (can read/write, struggles to speak)
- Device: Mobile-first (Android dominant)

**Primary Job to Be Done**  
Get confident enough in spoken English to pass a job interview, get promoted, or communicate with international clients — without spending money they don't have.

**Trigger Events**
- Got rejected from a job because of poor English speaking in interview
- Started a job requiring English communication and feels unprepared
- Wants to apply for a remote job at a foreign company
- Friend or colleague got promoted because of better English

**Top Pains**
1. *"I know grammar but I freeze when I have to speak"* — studied English for years in school but never practiced actual conversation
2. *"The apps that actually help cost more than I can spend"* — ELSA ($12/mo) and Speak ($15/mo) are out of reach
3. *"I have nobody to practice with"* — no English-speaking friends or community nearby
4. *"I don't know if I'm saying it right"* — no instant, specific feedback on what sounds wrong

**Desired Outcomes**
- Hold a 10-minute job interview in English without freezing
- Feel confident on calls with international colleagues
- Be able to explain their ideas clearly without embarrassing mistakes
- Get promoted or land a better-paying job

**Objections and Fears**
- "Will this actually help me speak, or is it just another quiz app?"
- "I've tried apps before and they didn't work"
- "What if I sound stupid to the AI?"
- Fear of judgment when making mistakes

**Alternatives They Consider**
- YouTube English channels (free but passive, no speaking practice)
- Duolingo (free but doesn't teach real speaking)
- HelloTalk (free but unstructured, awkward with strangers)
- Paying for a tutor (expensive, scheduling issues)

**Key Vocabulary**
- "practice speaking English"
- "English conversation practice free"
- "improve English for work"
- "speak English fluently"
- "English for interview"

**How to Reach Them**
- Channels: YouTube, TikTok, Facebook Groups, local forums (e.g., Voz for Brazil, Kaskus for Indonesia)
- Content they trust: "I went from B1 to fluent in 6 months" success stories, practical tips
- Communities: r/languagelearning, r/EnglishLearning, local Facebook English learning groups

## Persona 2 — The Career Upskiller ✓ VALIDATED
*Partially validated by real user interview #1 — software developer, HCMC, Vietnam (2026-05-27)*

**Profile**
- Age: 28–45
- Location: Any non-English country, often urban (validated: Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam)
- Occupation: Engineer, manager, nurse, researcher, entrepreneur (validated: software developer)
- Income: Middle class in their country
- English level: B1–B2 (functional written English, weak spoken — validated: understands but struggles to express)
- Device: Mix of mobile and desktop
- Daily English context: Microsoft Teams meetings, chat, email (validated)

**Primary Job to Be Done**  
Make English feel like instinct — not a skill to consciously apply — so they can focus on the work itself instead of the language during high-pressure professional situations like client meetings and calls.

> *"Tôi muốn dùng tiếng Anh như bản năng chứ không còn là kỹ năng nữa"*  
> *"I want to use English as instinct, not as a skill anymore"* — Interview #1

**Trigger Events**
- Embarrassing incident on a work call with international stakeholders
- Realizing clients don't understand them despite communicating daily (validated)
- Promotion opportunity that requires regular English meetings
- Company expanding to English-speaking markets
- Moving to an English-speaking country

**Top Pains** *(★ = validated by real user)*
1. ★ *"Long meetings drain my focus and I miss the full meaning"* — comprehension holds for short bursts but fails over time
2. ★ *"Clients sometimes don't understand me"* — caused by sentence structure, wrong vocabulary, or unclear pronunciation
3. ★ *"I don't know what tool to use to improve"* — discovery is a barrier, not price
4. *"I don't have time for a structured course"* — wants short sessions that fit lunch break or evenings (validated)
5. *"I've been learning on-the-job but I'm not actually improving"* — daily use reinforces habits, doesn't fix them

**⚠️ Assumption Overturned by Interview:**  
Original persona assumed **price** ("can't afford $12/mo") was the main barrier. Interview #1 never mentioned price once. The real barrier is **discovery** ("I don't know what to use") and **rationalization** ("I thought work was enough"). Revising P1 segment accordingly.

**Desired Outcomes**
- Speak in client meetings without mental translation — words come automatically
- Clients understand them clearly on first attempt
- Feel credible and confident, not anxious, during Teams calls
- English becomes a background tool, not the foreground challenge

**Ideal Product Behavior** *(from interview)*
- Available evenings after work and during lunch break
- Short sessions (15–20 min max)
- Repeated practice of real scenarios until they feel automatic
- Covers both work situations AND daily life — not just professional English

**Objections and Fears**
- ★ "I don't have time" — sessions must be short and immediately useful
- ★ "I don't know if this tool is right for me" — needs zero-friction onboarding
- "Will this fit into my schedule?"
- Worried about data privacy — work conversations are sensitive

**Alternatives They Consider** *(validated: none — user had tried nothing)*
- Nothing currently — **this is the most important finding**: working professionals who need English daily are not actively seeking solutions. LECA must reach them through discovery channels they already use, not through app store search.

**Key Vocabulary** *(user's own words)*
- "tiếng Anh như bản năng" (English as instinct)
- "giao tiếp với khách hàng nước ngoài" (communicate with foreign clients)
- "meeting bằng tiếng Anh" (English meetings)
- "luyện giao tiếp" (practice communication)

**How to Reach Them**
- Channels: LinkedIn, developer communities (Facebook groups for Vietnamese devs), company Slack/Teams
- Key insight: they are NOT actively searching for English learning tools — must reach them through content about career growth, remote work, or working with foreign clients
- Content that works: *"How I stopped losing clients in meetings"*, *"From B1 to fluent in 90 days while working full-time"*
- Communities: Professional Discord servers, LinkedIn groups, industry forums

## Persona 3 — The ESL Educator / Institution

**Profile**
- Role: English teacher, curriculum designer, ESL program director, NGO education coordinator
- Institution type: Language school, university ESL program, corporate L&D, government literacy program, international NGO
- Location: Global — highest density in Asia, Latin America, Middle East, Eastern Europe
- Scale: Manages 20–500+ learners

**Primary Job to Be Done**  
Give students real speaking practice at scale without requiring a 1-on-1 teacher for every learner — and do it within a constrained (often zero) technology budget.

**Trigger Events**
- Class sizes grew but staff didn't — 1:1 speaking practice is impossible
- Students are passing written exams but failing real-world communication
- Institution is adopting technology for the first time post-COVID
- Grant or government funding available for English learning technology
- Current vendor raised prices or ended their education discount

**Top Pains**
1. *"My students get zero speaking practice at home between classes"* — homework is passive (textbook, video), not productive
2. *"We can't afford ELSA or Cambly for 200 students"* — per-seat pricing is prohibitive at scale
3. *"Proprietary tools won't let me customize for my curriculum"* — need to add local vocabulary, specific scenarios, local language UI
4. *"I can't audit what the AI is teaching my students"* — black-box AI is a trust problem for educators
5. *"Data stays with the vendor"* — student data sovereignty is a compliance issue for institutions

**Desired Outcomes**
- Students get 30+ minutes of speaking practice per week outside of class
- Measurable improvement in speaking confidence and fluency over a semester
- Tool integrates with existing LMS (Moodle, Google Classroom)
- Can customize scenarios to match curriculum
- Free or affordable for the institution

**Objections and Fears**
- "Will students actually use it, or will they abandon it?"
- "Is the AI accurate enough that it won't teach bad habits?"
- "Can I self-host this for data privacy compliance?"
- "Does it support [local language] for L1 explanations?"

**Alternatives They Consider**
- ELSA for Business/Schools (expensive custom pricing)
- Speechling (limited AI)
- Building something themselves (too technical)
- Just assigning more textbook work (ineffective)

**Key Vocabulary**
- "speaking practice tool for ESL students"
- "AI for English language teaching"
- "open-source language learning"
- "self-hosted English app"
- "ESL classroom technology"

**How to Reach Them**
- Channels: TESOL conferences, teacher Facebook groups, ELT (English Language Teaching) journals, LinkedIn
- Content they trust: Peer-reviewed case studies, teacher community recommendations
- Communities: TESOL International, r/TEFL, local ELT associations

## Persona 4 — The Developer / Open-Source Contributor

**Profile**
- Age: 20–40
- Background: Software developer, ML engineer, NLP researcher, or language learning enthusiast with technical skills
- Location: Global — strong in India, Eastern Europe, Southeast Asia, Latin America
- Motivation: Mix of personal interest, resume building, language learning for self, belief in open-source

**Primary Job to Be Done**  
Contribute to or build on a high-quality open-source language learning platform — either to improve it for themselves, showcase their skills, or extend it for a specific use case.

**Trigger Events**
- Built their own Anki/Whisper/LLM script for language practice, wants something more structured to contribute to
- Searched for an open-source alternative to ELSA/Speak and found nothing
- Works at a language school or edtech company and wants to evaluate the codebase
- Wants to build a language-specific extension (their native language + English)

**Top Pains**
1. *"I want AI conversation practice but I don't want to pay $15/mo for a black box"* — technically capable, prefers open-source
2. *"There's nothing to contribute to — all the good language apps are closed source"*
3. *"I want to add [feature/language] but no existing app lets me"*
4. *"I distrust AI apps that won't show me their prompt or model"*

**Desired Outcomes**
- Working local development environment in under 30 minutes
- Clear contribution guidelines and welcoming community
- Modular architecture they can extend (add a new language, scenario, or AI model)
- Recognition in the community (GitHub stars, contributors list)

**Objections and Fears**
- "Is this project actively maintained?"
- "Is the architecture good enough to build on?"
- "Is the community welcoming to newcomers?"

**Alternatives They Consider**
- Building their own from scratch (high effort)
- Contributing to adjacent OSS projects (LangChain, Whisper, Hugging Face)
- Using the OpenAI API directly with a custom prompt

**Key Vocabulary**
- "open-source language learning"
- "Whisper + LLM conversation"
- "AI English tutor self-hosted"
- "contribute to EdTech open source"

**How to Reach Them**
- Channels: GitHub, Hacker News, r/MachineLearning, Discord OSS communities, Dev.to
- Content they trust: Technical READMEs, architecture deep-dives, demo videos
- Key signal: Star the repo → open an issue → first PR
